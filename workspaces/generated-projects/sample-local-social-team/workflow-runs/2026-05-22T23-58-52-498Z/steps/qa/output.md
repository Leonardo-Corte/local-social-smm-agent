## QA Recap

### Passes
- The workspace includes agent roles, calendar, drafts, visual briefs, source registry, publishing checklist, persona report, and memory.
- Publishing is human-approved by default.

### Not Good Enough Yet
1. **Strategic Fit**
   - Niche clarity: While the niche of local-first AI tools for creators and small businesses is clear, the offer (a local-first AI social media workspace factory) could be made more compelling with specific benefits that address target audience pain points.
   - Target clarity: The project targets solo creators, small agencies, and founders who want professional social content without recurring SaaS costs. However, there are no concrete examples or case studies to demonstrate the value proposition for these segments.

2. **Content Quality**
   - Hooks: Although hooks like "Stop paying for a social stack before your content system is even working" are sharp and relevant, they need more specific context and proof points.
   - Drafts match voice: The draft content matches the tone of being practical and slightly bold but lacks concrete examples or before/after workflows to substantiate claims.

3. **Local Feasibility**
   - Model profile realism: The model routing context is accurate for a local-first solution, but there's no benchmarking against other solutions to validate its effectiveness.
   - Visual workload matches hardware: The visual workload is optional and human-approved before publishing, aligning with the constraints. However, no concrete proof of concept visuals are provided.

4. **Platform Safety**
   - No spam automation: Correctly set up as per the policy, but needs verification for each generated artifact to ensure compliance.
   - Approval gate present: The publishing is indeed gated by human approval, but explicit steps in the checklist need to be followed before approving any content.

5. **Actionability**
   - Calendar completeness: While a calendar is included, it does not contain specific dates or tasks that align with the persona reports’ requirements and conversion triggers.
   - Publishing checklist usability: The checklist needs more detailed actions linked directly to the proof points and examples required by each segment of the target audience.

- **Strategic Fit**

- **Content Quality**
  - Incorporate specific before/after workflow artifacts and proof points in the draft content to support the claims made about improving workflows for target audiences.

- **Local Feasibility**
  - Include at least one concrete visual or screenshot that demonstrates how the local-first AI social media workspace factory works, providing a tangible example of its capabilities.
  
- **Platform Safety**
  - Ensure each generated artifact is manually reviewed against the platform safety checklist before publishing to avoid any potential policy violations.

- **Actionability**
  - Populate the calendar with specific tasks and due dates that align with the persona reports’ conversion triggers. For instance, schedule a task for generating a concrete proof point for the Skeptical operator by a certain date.
  
  - Ensure the publishing checklist includes actionable steps such as:
    - Reviewing content against the strategic fit and target audience needs.
    - Verifying local feasibility through model benchmarking or similar validation.
    - Finalizing action items in the calendar.
