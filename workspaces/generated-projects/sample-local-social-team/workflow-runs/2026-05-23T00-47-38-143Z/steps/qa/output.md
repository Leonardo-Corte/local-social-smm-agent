## QA Recap

### Passes
- The workspace includes agent roles, calendar, drafts, visual briefs, source registry, publishing checklist, persona report, and memory.
- Publishing is human-approved by default.

### Not Good Enough Yet
- Research content lacks depth and needs to be based on live trend collection once implemented.
- Draft posts are placeholders and need refinement for final campaign quality.
- Model routing decisions rely solely on profile-based assessments rather than comprehensive benchmarking.

### Weaknesses Identified

#### Strategic Fit
1. **Offer and Goal Clarity**: The offer is clear but the specific business outcome needs more detail to ensure alignment with target audience segments.
2. **Content Pillars**: Content pillars are not explicitly mapped to a detailed business strategy, making it harder for the audience to see how their use of this tool leads to success.

#### Content Quality
1. **Hooks and Drafts**: Hooks in draft posts need refinement to be more engaging and specific to target segments.
2. **Visual Formats**: Visual formats vary but lack high-quality, brand-specific variants that could better attract attention.

#### Local Feasibility
- The profile-based model routing needs to be updated with a benchmarked approach to ensure the best models are used effectively on local hardware.

#### Platform Safety
1. **Approval Gates and Risk Management**: While approval gates are present, more detailed risk management and source labeling need to be added.
2. **Risky Modules**: Ensure that all risky modules remain disabled as per the publishing policy.

#### Actionability
- The calendar and checklist need clearer definitions of tasks and deadlines for actionability.
- Regeneration tasks should include specific steps for each persona’s feedback points.
