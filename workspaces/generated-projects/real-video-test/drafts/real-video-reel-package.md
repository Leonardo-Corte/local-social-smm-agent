## Editing Improvements

### Structure

1. Keep the visible brand overlay in the first second.
2. Cut quickly from entrance/crowd to bar action.
3. Use the food tray shot as a rhythm break.
4. Hold the orange cocktail hero shot slightly longer at the end.
5. End with a clean text card only if there is a confirmed next event or official CTA.

### Timing

- 0.0-1.2s: title/brand overlay.
- 1.2-3.5s: crowd and entrance energy.
- 3.5-6.5s: bar/staff/service movement.
- 6.5-8.5s: food tray/detail shot.
- 8.5-11.2s: cocktail hero shot and final text.

### Sound Direction

- Use upbeat lounge/house, afro-house, disco-house, or warm funk edits.
- Avoid overly aggressive EDM; the visuals feel premium/social, not club chaos.
- Cut on visible movement: crowd motion, staff hand movement, tray movement, cocktail reveal.
- If original ambient audio is clean, keep a very low room-tone layer under the music for authenticity.
- If the original audio is noisy, mute it and use music only.

### Text Overlay

- Use 3-5 words max per overlay.
- Keep text away from faces and cocktail glass.
- Prefer one main line at the top third and a small location/collab line at the bottom.
- Do not add claims like `sold out`, `exclusive`, `best night`, or attendance numbers unless confirmed.

### Color And Finish

- Slight warmth is good because the venue lighting already supports it.
- Add mild contrast, not heavy saturation.
- Protect skin tones and orange cocktail color.
- Avoid darkening the crowd too much; social proof depends on the room being readable.
